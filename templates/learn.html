Objective : The primary goal of chess is to checkmate the opponent's king, meaning the king is under attack and cannot escape capture.

The Board: Chess is played on an 8x8 grid with alternating light and dark squares. Each player begins with 16 pieces: 1 king, 1 queen, 2 rooks, 2 bishops, 2 knights, and 8 pawns.

Pieces

The King

King is the most important piece. It moves one square in any direction (horizontal, vertical, or diagonal).

The Queen

The queen is the most powerful piece, capable of moving in any direction over any number of squares vertically, horizontally, or diagonally.

The Bishop

A bishop moves diagonally and starts between the king's knight and the king or between the queen's knight and the queen.

The Knight

The knight moves in an "L" shape and is the only piece that can jump over others.

The Rook

The rook moves any number of squares horizontally or vertically.

The Pawn

Pawns move forward one square but capture diagonally. They can move two squares on their first move.

Game Insights

Points distribution based on eliminating opponent pieces:

  • Soldier: 1 point
  • Bishop: 3 points
  • Knight: 3 points
  • Rook: 5 points
  • Queen: 8 points

Extra points given based on game results:

  • Win: gain 9 points
  • Draw: 0 points
  • Loss: lose 9 points

Check and Checkmate:

  • Check: The king is under direct attack by an opponent's piece.
  • Checkmate: The king is in check and there is no legal move to escape.

Draws:

  • Stalemate: If a player has no legal moves and their king is not in check, the game is a draw.
  • Insufficient Material: If neither player has enough pieces to checkmate (e.g., only kings left), the game is a draw.
